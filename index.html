The Rowhammer Bug

Rowhammer is a vulnerability in DRAM devices that allows for attacks such as privilege escalation and sandbox escaping. Repeatedly accessing a row in recent DRAM devices can cause bit flips in adjacent rows, and attacks demonstrated and documented by the Google Project Zero team have used this behavior to gain kernel prileges on x86-64 Linux machines (from unprivileged user-land).

The x86 exploit utilizes repeated CLFUSH instructions to induce bit-flipping and gain read-write access to all of physical memory. However, the Google team believes this vulnerability to exist on other machines and operating systems. This is because the vulnerability is a result of the increasing density of DRAM devices; since the individual cells are packed closer together, the electrical charge sent to one row has a greater chance of effecting the cells in adjacent rows as well.
